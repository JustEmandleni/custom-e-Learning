  2. a) four observations
    • Populations can produce far more offspring than needed ü
    • Sizes of most natural population and resources remain relatively constant ü. 
    • Natural variation of characteristics among members of the same species are evident ü
    • Some characteristics are inherited and so are passed on to the next generation ü

    b) Organisms produce a large number of offspring ü.
    There is a lot of variation among offspring as offspring differ from their parents in minor random ways – for example, in the length of their necks ü.
    The offspring with traits / characteristics that make them better suited to the environment – i.e. with long necks to get to food sources others can’t get to – will be most likely to survive and reproduce ü.
    The organisms without the trait are less able to get to the food and so will die ü.
    This means that more offspring in the next generation will have longer necks, a helpful difference. The next generation will have a higher proportion of individuals with the new trait – long necks ü.
    These differences – the long necks – accumulate and eventually all individuals in a population have the new trait ü
    Over time, together with other minor changes, this process leads to an entirely new species and evolution by natural selection will have take place ü.
