Mendel as father of genetics

Gregor Mendel, an Austrian monk (a type of priest), is regarded as the father of genetics for his work on garden pea plants that helped explain how genes are passed from parents to offspring.

Mendel’s work on the genetics of peas began with the observation of peas to determine what traits were inherited. He noticed at least 7 traits that appeared to be inherited.

These traits were (see Table 2):

  • seed shape

  • seed colour

  • pod shape (pod – the container holding a plant’s seeds)

  • pod colour

  • flower colour

  • flower position (whether axial – on the side, or terminal – on top)

  • stem length
