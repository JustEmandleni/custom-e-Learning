Negative Feedback

A negative feedback mechanism is an interaction between two hormones in which one hormone stimulates an increase in another hormone which then inhibits the first hormone, thus restoring balance.

The following is the general sequence of events in a negative feedback mechanism:

  • an imbalance is detected by the receptor

  • a control centre is stimulated

  • the control centre responds

  • a message sent to target organ/s which are the effectors

  • the effector responds

  • it opposes (reverses) the imbalance

  • balance is restored

Figure 7: Homeostasis mechanism

As illustrated in Figure 7, an imbalance triggers a stimulus to the receptors at 2. When stimulated, the receptors generate an impulse that is transmitted to the control centre at 3. The control centre responds by sending an impulse to the effector which is the target organ at 4. The target organ then reverses the imbalance and balance is restored at 5.
