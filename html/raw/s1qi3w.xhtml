Activity 1: Fertilisation

1. External fertilisation  ü

2. The fish are close to each other when they release their gametes ü  . Many egg and sperm cells are released to maximise the chances of fertilisation ü  .

3. Oviparous / Ovipary  ü

4. The eggs are already in water ü  , no desiccation (drying-out) will occur ü .

5. The chances of fertilisation are reduced as gametes have to meet in water ü  . To overcome this challenge, many gametes are produced and released shortly after each other and while the parents are close to each other to maximise the chances of fertilisation ü  .

Offspring do not have a large degree of protection from the external environment and are easily predated upon ü  . To overcome this challenge, parents can offer parental care and defend eggs and juveniles. Offspring are also precocially developed and well-suited for their environment meaning that they can fend for themselves ü  .
