Synapse

A synapse (see Figure 7 below) is a functional connection between the axon of one neuron and the dendrites of another neuron. The connection is established by neurotransmitters, chemicals that move across the gap between axon and dendrite to transmit the impulse.

Significance of a synapse

  • It ensures that the impulse moves in one direction only

  • It prevents continuous stimulation of the neurons

  • It ensures that the impulse is transmitted from the sensory neuron to the motor neuron
