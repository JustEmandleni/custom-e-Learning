4. The process of DNA replication

  • Double helix DNA unwinds as the weak hydrogen bonds between the nitrogenous bases break ü

  • The two DNA strands separate from each other (DNA unzips)and each original DNA strand serves as a template to form a new strandü

  • Free DNA nucleotides from the nucleoplasm attach to the original strandü

  • The nucleotides attach to their complementary bases (A to T and G to C)ü

  • Each DNA molecule now consists of one original strand and one new strandü

  • The result is two genetically identical DNA molecules; the entire process is controlled by enzymesü
