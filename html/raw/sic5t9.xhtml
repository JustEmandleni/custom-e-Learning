Activity 4: Out of Africa hypothesis

  1. All modern humans (Homo sapiens) ü originated in Africa  ü and migrated to other parts of the world  ü

  2. It is a hypothesis, since it relies on currently available fossil evidence that has been studied and from which relationships etc. are inferred ü. Fossil evidence yet to be discovered can contradict the presently generally accepted Out of Africa hypothesis ü . This has happened a few times in the past. Some scientists argue for a multi-regional origin ü . A theory is more definitive than a hypothesis ü , hence the Out of Africa hypothesis, not theory.

  3. Genetic evidence  ü – mitochondrial DNA is inherited from the mother ü . Analysis of mutations / markers on the mtDNA shows the oldest female ancestors  ü were living in Africa ü , and that all currently living humans are descended from her ü .
    Fossil evidence  ü – the fossils for Ardipithecus, for Australopithecus and for Homo habilis, have been found only in Africa ü . The oldest Homo erectus fossil  ü and the oldest Homo sapiens fossil  ü have also been found in Africa.

  4. Mitochondrial DNA is the small circular chromosome  ü found inside mitochondria ü . The mitochondria, and thus mitochondrial DNA, are passed only from mother to offspring through the egg cell ü .

  5. Scientists look for mutations in the mtDNA ü . The mutations (called markers) occur at a steady and known rate ü . Those with the greatest number of markers are the oldest human populations (all from Africa)  ü . Comparing markers allows scientists also to determined when populations separated from each other ü .

  6. The importance of the Cradle of Humankind derives from the abundance of hominin fossil (40% of all world-wide) ü found here. The fossil species found only here ü : Australopithecus africanus, Paranthropus ü . The first African fossils of Homo erectus were found here ü .

  7. Makapansgat Valley ü : this cave complex has a significant fossil record, and 40 individuals of the species A. africanus that is only found in South Africa ü .
    Florisbad ü : an almost complete skull of an early Homo sapiens (or of H. heidelbergensis) – with a cranial volume of 1400 mL – was found there ü .
